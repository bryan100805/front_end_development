My Journey in SP

My Experience in SP

When I just started studying in SP, it was quite scary as it was a completely new environment for me. My lecturers, my classmates, the classrooms and even the foodcourts were all different to me compared to in Secondary School. Workload in School of Computing was also very overwhelming. However, this quickly took a big turn as I managed to make a few friends in my class and also catch up in my studies. I even joined 2 CCAs, Muay Thai, where I learnt different combat techniques, both attacking and defence, & my other cca, SPAI , where I learn additional AI and programming skills and content to get me more familiar on the topic of AI. This allowed me to socialise with people from other schools and different years in SP, like School of Engineering and School of Media, Arts and Design. I would say that SP offers a lot of food options across the campus, they have 6 food courts, 2 cafes and even an Old Chang Kee food truck, which means that I will never run out of food choices to eat. One of the food court even has a Starbucks.

I would say one thing that stand out to me in SP compared to in secondary school is that everything need to be done independently. For example, I need to keep track of all of my homework by myself, without any reminders from the teachers/lecturers.

Courses in SOC:

DAAA

From Siri to Google Home and online chatbots, data analytics and AI make it possible for devices and programmes to respond to us in an almost human-like manner. Diploma in Applied AI & Analytics (DAAA) offers modules like Data Engineering, Machine Learning and Deep Learning to help students pursue their passion as a data analyst, data engineer or an AI practitioner.

The course has specially designed AI computer labs which provide students with high performing computers. The lab is also equipped with AI Deep Learning servers capable of handling heavy lifting machine learning task.

DISM

The threat has never been so real. The more we rely on the Internet to store and manage important information, the more we need to keep it safe from cybercriminals. Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) students can can look forward to an interesting curriculum that covers offensive attacks, defensive methods and investigative skills.

To prepare students for both further studies and working life, the DISM curriculum covers the important areas of security technology, security management, information technology, law and teamwork and communications. Cyber Wargame Centre is even created to simulate a real life Hacker vs Security Analyst scenario, in which students will try to prevent these cyberthreats from occuring.

DIT

Information Technology (IT) is the driving force behind many digital transformations we see and use today, including the Smart Nation vision. In Singapore, there is a strong demand for IT professionals in Software Development and User Experience (UX) design, and in Immersive Simulation Technologies such as Augmented and Virtual Reality. Diploma in Information Technology (DIT) focus on Software & Applications development such as Software Development, User Experience (UX) & Immersive Simulation.

Students may be offered to work with leading industry companies such as Microsoft, Accenture, GovTech, CrimsonLogic, KPMG, Singtel, FWD Insurance, DBS, UOB, Associates Consulting and Ubisoft Singapore, through internship opportunities and project collaborations. Students can pursue an IT-related degree in both local and overseas universities with advanced standing.

DCTIP

The Common ICT Programme (DCITP) is designed to help students to make an informed choice if they are unsure about what IT courses they need to take.
The DCITP is a semester long programme designed to give students a broad introduction to the Infocomm landscape and help them gain insights into the occupations and job roles, career pathways and employment opportunities in the various Infocomm sectors. The DCITP will also help students lay a strong foundation in programming and IT fundamentals.

At the end of the year 1 semester 1, they will be able make an informed choice in selecting one of the three IT courses that you wish to pursue:

  1. Diploma in Applied AI & Analytics
    (DAAA - S30)
  2. Diploma in Information Technology
    (DIT - S69)
  3. Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM - S54)
